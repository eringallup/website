the project

This project was a sketch to test out the concept of a ‘Tinder for jobs’. The first task was to decide on a name and pull together a logo, which would give us a starting point for the design.

The name and logo are designed to evoke trust and hint at exclusivity.

the app : home

The app is designed for the job seeker needing a low friction, discrete way of showing interest in new opportunities. Upon sign up the user enters the url of their Linkedin profile, which allows the app to scan keywords from their profile to pre-qualify them for posted jobs.

the app : preferences

The seeker sets their preferences to filter their mandatory requirements like team size, commute time, company type and perks.

the app : posted job

The user can then browse positions that are a match for them. A short, tidy overview area keeps descriptions succinct.

the app : swiping up

Swiping up gives more details of the team and office location.

Instead of responsibilites, projects are listed to give the seeker a more exact idea of what they would be doing.

the app : company info & perks

Financial information and leadership links allow the user to browse deeper if they'd like to know more about the company.

Perks include the must-haves already selected by the seeker in preferences.

Who are

passive job seekers?

The passive job seeker is a skilled employee who is starting to feel that the next job is on the horizon. They are busy and do not have the time to comb job sites and wade through cryptic job descriptions. They only want to see the jobs that they'd leave their current job for. And most of all, they do not want to be approached by recruiters – unless it is a true match.
